Kà màstai?
<br>
<br>
Instaliacija Trakø Vokës dvaro rûsyje. 
<br>
<br>
Pamaèius rûsyje árengtà koplytëlæ - be ðviesos, apleistà, lyg ir ðventà vietà, bet kartu nesukelianèià 
dieviðkøjø jausmø - kyla klausimas, ar tai nëra fikcija? Kiek dvare iðliko tikro dvaro, kiek iðsaugota 
autentiðkos aplinkos, o kiek jame yra parodijos á dvarà? Kûrinis „Kà màstai?“ – tiesioginis klausimas þiûrovui. 
Kaip jis vertina tai, kà mato, Kokia ðiandien yra koplyèia dvare ir koks yra pats dvaras? Kaip ðiandien þiûrime á religijà, 
tikëjimà, ðventas vietas? Kas atrodo svarbu, o ko nesureikðminame? Ðis kûrinis tai ne atsakymas á klausimà, ne vertinimas, o bandymas apmàstyti, 
kaip vertinti veiksmà „pasûdyti maistà ðventa druska“. Nors gal jo vertinti nereikia, gal pagalvoti kodël druska su þvake atsirado ant stalo ir 
kokia istorija uþ to slypi?

<br>

<br>
What do you think?
<br>
<br>
Installation in the basement of Traku Vokes Manor
<br>
<br>
When you see this derelict without light Chapel in the basement you will probably think, isn‘t it a fiction?
 How much authentic atmosphere is left and how much parody of the estate is here? The artwork „What do you think“ 
is the direct question to viewers. How do they assess that they see at the estate? What do religion, faith, holy places mean to us today? 
What is important to us and what don't we overestimate? The artwork isn‘t an answer or an appraisal. It’s an effort to understand, how to interpret
 the action „to salt your food by Holy salt“. Maybe it isn‘t necessary to assess this action, maybe we have to think WHY salt ant the candle are on the table,
 what‘s a story behind this?
<br>
<br>
Anastasija Kodzisova (Lithuania, 1995) is a young Lithuanian artist. She has graduated from the Vilnius Academy of Arts with BA (2017) and MA (2019) degrees
 in painting. She is interested in invisible border between people and the definition „we and the others”. 2017 – Special rating commission reward given in 
graduating Works contest by ethnic minorities department of Lithuanian Republic for BA art Project „My grandmother was born in Mogiliov, I was born in Vilnius“.
